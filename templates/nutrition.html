Macros

Macros are very important to know in order to effectively understand how much nutrients your body needs to build muscle or loose fat. Without fuelling your body with the correct macros it will take significantly more time to reach your goals.

Macro stands for macro nutrient and it is the proportion of which your overall daily Kcals are broken down into. Macros are split into 3 sections of nutrients, Carbs, Proteins and fats. Its the amount of each of these nutrients added together that makes your over all daily calories. Dependant on your goals, macros should be portioned out to ensure you are giving your body enough calories to fuel your it through training days and not to much for rest days.

Carb cycling

People often have a bad relationship with carbs, not knowing wether they are good or bad or wether they should be eating low GI or high GI. Simply, carbs are essential to training and both low and high GI carbs have there place in a good diet. Cutting out carbs is cutting your bodies vital supply of energy causing a negative effect on both training and lifestyle. Carb cycling allows your body to utilise carbs and energy in the most effective way and also ensures you don't have to much excess that could potentially be stored as fat.
